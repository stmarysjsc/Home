About Our Church

“With the abundant blessings of God Almighty, our parish continues to grow in faith, fellowship, and service.”

Our History

Beginnings

The history of “St. Mary’s Jacobite Syrian Church, Naigaon – Vasai” is connected to the history of “St. Thomas Syrian Church Congregation, Byculla, Mumbai” which was established in the year 1975. As the number of Jacobite Syrian Christian families increased, many members came and settled in Mira Road, Bhayandar, Naigaon, Vasai, Nallasopara and Virar. However, for Holy Qurbana and other spiritual needs, they depended on Byculla and Malad parishes.

Foundation of the Church

A service center was setup and the first Holy Qurbana was conducted on 29th November,1987 by Rev. Fr. Varghese Valayil. With growing members, Vasai was declared an independent center in 1998. Later, with the efforts of parishioners, land of 65 cents was donated by Mr. Antony John Pereira.

Growth of the Parish

On 13th September, 1999, the foundation stone of the church was consecrated by H. G. Thomas Mor Themotheos. On 10th April, 2000, the Altar stone was blessed by H. H. Moran Mor Ignatius Zakka I, Patriarch of Antioch. The Holy Mooron Consecration of the new Church was conducted in June 2002. A library was started in 2002, which now has over 5000 books.

Major Milestones

  • First Holy Qurbana – 29th November, 1987
  • Foundation Stone consecrated – 13th September, 1999
  • Altar Stone blessed by Patriarch Ignatius Zakka I – April 2000
  • Holy Mooron Consecration – June 2002
  • Fr. Abraham Padanilathu Memorial Library inaugurated – September 2002
  • Service Center at Nallasopara established – January 2001
  • Consecration of Nallasopara center – June 2008
  • Kurishumthotti dedicated to Mother Mary – September 2005

Present

Today, the parish has more than 100 families. Services are held every Sunday at St. Mary’s, Naigaon, and at St. George Service Center, Nallasopara. Sunday School, Prayer meetings, Parish Youth Association and Martha Mariam Vanitha Samajam are actively functioning. Our church is also blessed with its own cemetery, the only Jacobite Church in Mumbai to have one. We look forward to expanding our charitable work with new initiatives.
